Types of Waste

Understanding different waste categories is the first step toward effective waste management. Learn how to identify and properly handle each type of waste.

Organic Waste

Organic Waste

Biodegradable waste from plants and animals that can decompose naturally. This is the most common type of household waste.

Common Examples:

  • Food scraps and leftovers
  • Fruit and vegetable peels
  • Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • Eggshells
  • Garden waste (leaves, grass clippings)
  • Paper towels and napkins

Proper Disposal:

Can be composted or used for biogas production. Never mix with other waste types.

Recyclable Materials

Recyclable Materials

Materials that can be processed and transformed into new products, reducing the need for raw materials.

Common Examples:

  • Paper and cardboard
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Plastic containers
  • Metal cans (aluminum, steel)
  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Clean packaging materials

Proper Disposal:

Clean and sort by material type. Check local recycling guidelines.

Electronic Waste (E-Waste)

Electronic Waste (E-Waste)

Discarded electrical or electronic devices. Contains valuable materials but also hazardous substances.

Common Examples:

  • Old computers and laptops
  • Mobile phones and tablets
  • Televisions and monitors
  • Batteries
  • Small appliances
  • Cables and chargers

Proper Disposal:

Take to designated e-waste collection centers. Never dispose in regular trash.

Hazardous Waste

Hazardous Waste

Waste that poses substantial or potential threats to public health or the environment.

Common Examples:

  • Paints and solvents
  • Cleaning chemicals
  • Pesticides and herbicides
  • Motor oil and automotive fluids
  • Fluorescent bulbs
  • Medical waste

Proper Disposal:

Requires special handling. Contact local hazardous waste facilities.

Quick Identification Tips

Check Labels

Look for recycling symbols and disposal instructions on packaging.

When in Doubt

Contact local waste management authorities for guidance.

Keep It Clean

Rinse recyclables to remove food residue.

Separate Early

Use separate bins to make waste sorting easier.
