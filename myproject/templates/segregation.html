Waste Segregation & Recycling

Master the art of waste segregation with our comprehensive step-by-step guide. Proper sorting is essential for effective recycling and environmental protection.

5-Step Segregation Process

Step 1

Set Up Separate Bins

  • Use color-coded bins
  • Place bins in convenient locations
  • Ensure bins are properly sized
  • Add clear pictorial labels
Step 2

Clean and Prepare Items

  • Rinse food containers
  • Remove caps and labels
  • Flatten cardboard boxes
  • Dry items before recycling
Step 3

Sort by Material Type

  • Paper & cardboard together
  • Plastics by recycling code
  • Glass bottles and jars
  • Metal cans separately
Step 4

Handle Special Items

  • Store e-waste separately
  • Keep hazardous waste safe
  • Bag organic waste
  • Never mix waste categories
Step 5

Schedule Regular Disposal

  • Follow local collection schedules
  • Use recycling centers if needed
  • Dispose hazardous waste properly
  • Compost organic waste

What Can Be Recycled?

Paper & Cardboard

✔ Accepted Items

  • Newspapers
  • Magazines
  • Office paper
  • Cardboard boxes
  • Paper bags

✖ Not Accepted

  • Waxed paper
  • Tissue paper
  • Paper towels
  • Greasy pizza boxes
Plastics

✔ Accepted Items

  • Bottles (#1, #2)
  • Containers (#5)
  • Rigid plastics

✖ Not Accepted

  • Plastic bags
  • Styrofoam
  • Plastic wrap
Glass

✔ Accepted Items

  • Bottles
  • Jars
  • Clear & colored glass

✖ Not Accepted

  • Window glass
  • Mirrors
  • Ceramics
Metals

✔ Accepted Items

  • Aluminum cans
  • Steel cans
  • Clean foil
  • Metal lids

✖ Not Accepted

  • Aerosol cans
  • Paint cans
  • Hangers
